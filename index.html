My First Webpage

I read a paper titled “Gender stereotypes and academic performance: the influence of salient role models on stereotype validation”. The paper talks about how people in general feel the need to have a role model as a north star, especially when these models have proven track record of success in related fields. From the paper I was able to broaden my horizon on the issue of negative stereotype. I was exposed to how prejudice against women as regards intellectual capabilities could in fact fuel the paradigm of incompetence, which in turn makes a woman feel as though she can’t excel in certain professional fields especially the ones that are male dominated. The paper went on to speak about the existence of social narratives backing these false claims which in the end leads to a woman becoming more and more complaisant to(with maybe??) failure, not trusting in her own abilities and ultimately fitting into the status quo. Another part of the research that enthralled me was how gender specific role models could have a direct impact on competence, it spoke about how women tend to be more optimistic if the path they have chosen has been conquered be a fellow woman, it was stated in the paper that a female role model who has been able to succeed in a male domain is critical as evidence against negative stereotypes ultimately resulting in a higher sense of self-worth for aspiring women. The research then went on to give detailed analysis that proved the thesis and I must say that going through the research was a really enlightening experience for me.
